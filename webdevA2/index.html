Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart


~~The King of Classical Music~~

Widely known as one of the best musicians of history, known for his works as a composer for orchestras and countless impressive piano sonatas his rapid profiency in music resulted in him composing more than 800 works of music until his passing at 35 years old. His work is held in high regards through history.

Life

Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart was born on 27th January 1756 in Austria. He was a musical prodigy whom started composing and performing for royalty by age 5. Mozart died in 1791 at the age of 35, he wrote over 600 works across many genres including piano pieces throughout his life leading up to his death leaving his final work The Requiem largely unfinished.

Music
  • Sonata facile

    Sonata facile or known as Piano Sonata No.16 or Sonata semplice is described by Mozart himself as "For beginners" in his thematic catalouge it was added to his works on June 26th 1788 and was not published in his lifetime despite being well known, the piece was published in 1805 after his passing.

    Ronda Alla Turca

    Otherwise known as Piano Sonata No.11, composed in 1783 and published in 1784 Ronda Alla Turca is known as one of Mozart's greatest and well known piano pieces ever composed. known for its bouncy and uplifitng rythm. The piece imitates the sound of Turkish Janissary Bands which was much popular during Mozart's time

    Piano Sonata No.10

    Composed in 1783 and published in 1784 Sonata No.10 is also one of Mozart's highly commended pieces. it is one of the 3 works in the cycle of piano sonatas which included Sonata 11 and 12. in the third movement of the piece the last few bars of the music sheet are lost to history.

Ludwig Van Beethoven


~~Deaf To All But The Song~~

One of the most revered pianists in history the German composer and pianist Beethoven is the composer of many incredibly famous and well known pieces in piano history, such as the Moonlight Sonata, Für Elise and Sonata Opus 13. He is also well known for composing many amazing pieces while going deaf during the later times of his life.

Life

Ludwig Van Beethoven was born on 16th December 1770 in Bonn, Germany. He displayed his musical talents at a young age and was taught intensely by his father, Johann Van Beethoven and Christian Gotlob Neefe later on. He would compose his first piece at 11 years old, the Nine Variations on a march WoO 63 in 1782 which was published the same year. towards the later years of his life Beethoven grew increasingly deaf but would still continue to compose and conduct using creative tatics to continue writing music. The last piece that Beethoven composed was String Quartet No. 16 in F major, Op. 135 in 1826 a year later he would pass on in 1827 at 56 years old due to illness.

Music
  • Moonlight Sonata Movement 3

    The Moonlight Sonata is one of the most reconizeable piano pieces in the world. Its intense and fast paced movements mixed with its graceful slow down times creates one of intricately crafted pieces in the world. This section focuses on specifically the 3rd movement of the Moonlight Sonata as it is more widely known.

    Für Elise

    Für Elise is another one of Beethoven's very popular pieces. The piece was not published until 1867, 40 years after Beethoven's passing. Unfortuanetly the orginial piece has been lost to history. This piece is very well known as a beginners piece and is also known for its really reconizeable and elegant melody.

    Sonata Pathétique

    Sonata Pathétique or known as Piano Sonata No.8 is known as a tricky piece to play to many pianists. the sucess of this sonata was important to Beethoven's career to cement this reputation as a composer as it exposed the world to Beethoven's growing talent.

Frédéric François Chopin


~~Virtuist of Harmonic Resonance~~

Known as the leading composer of his era, quoted to be "poetic genius was based on a professional technique that was without equal in his generation" . despite being in poor health for most of his life, Chopin still managed to create a collage of memoriable meloidies that will forver be inprinted in musical history.

Life

Born in March 1st 1810 in Warsaw Frédéric was a child prodigy who finished his music education and composed his early works before leaving and settling in Paris where he would remaind until his death in 1849 at age 39. During his time at Paris he supported himself by teaching piano and selling his compositions and gave 30 live performances. During the final years of his life he was supported by Jane Stirling his admirer, until his death.

Music
  • Nocturnes, Op. 9

    Nocturnes, Op. 9 was written and dedicated to Marie Payel, whom was another well known musician during Chopin's time. This is one of Chopin's better known Nocturnes, being easily reconizeableby its calm, somber tone and its rythmic freedom. Said Nocturne has been popular recently from being featured as background music in many TikToks.

    Piano Sonata No. 2

    More well known as the Marche funebre (Funeral March), it is known as one of the best sonatas in history. Carrying allusions of Bach and Beethoven, although initial critisims in the beginning of its publishing the piece has gone down as one of Chopin's most well documented and most played pieces in history.

    Fantasie Inpromptu

    Written in 1834. Despite being one of Chopin's other well known works, Chopin never released Fantasie Inpromptu. Although not fully confirmed, The orginial manuscript noted that Chopin composed the piece had been composed and sold it to the Baroness, Baroness d'Este. This piece is known for its intense and elegant melody and technique used in playing it.

History

From royal residences to rock shows, the piano has continued to be one of the most potent and expressive instruments throughout history. Created by Bartolomeo Cristofori circa 1700, it was the first keyboard capable of producing both soft and loud sounds—transforming music. Legends such as Mozart and Beethoven transformed it into an expressive medium of narrative and intensity. With the advancement of technology, the piano transformed—from grand performance venues to contemporary synthesizers. Despite the passage of time, it perseveres as the "king of instruments," linking emotion to melody.

Early History 1400-1700:

The Piano evovled from other instuments such as the hapsicord and clavichold. Around 1700, Bartolomeo Cristofori was employed by The Grand Prince of Tuscany as the Keeper of Instruments. His knowledge of the workings of hapsicords and keyboard instruments helped Cristofori invent the first ever Pianos in history. Although we do not know when Cristofori first created the Piano, 3 original Cristofori pianos survive today dated from the 1720s

Cristofori called the piano: "gravicembalo col piano e forte", meaning "harpsichord with soft and loud." as it was the first keyboard instrument that could change its volume based on how hard it was played.

Classical Era 1700

The Classical Era was where the piano starte gaining its traction and was where brilliant musicians such as Mozart and Beethoven began to show the brilliance of the piano to the world by composing some of the most memorable pieces that would go down in the history of music. This was the true begenning of a revlation, the era of where one of the most brilliant instruments ever made started leaving its footprints in the sands of human history.

Romantic Era 1800

As the years went on, the piano evovled and so did its popularity. Eventually it became core instuments for concerts and homes, now known one of the most important instruments in western music as technological advancements is made to the piano such as making it more powerful and adding an iron frame to make it more sturdy. During this time musicians such as Chopin and Liszt started to display their talents on the piano and pushed it to its limits.

Modern Era 1900s onward

During the 1900s the piano was still on its peak in history, still being one of the most well known instruments in history. Eventually the modern Grand Piano was completed and much later on the Electric piano was created. The piano started to grow in popularity outside of the west and started spreading its influence into the east starting with Japan during the Meji era . Eventually in the 1920s, the piano replaced the Koto as the preffered instrument for Japanese women to learn before marriage as the piano became a symbol of domestic modernity.

One known Japanese pianist in the Meji Era was Rentaro Taki who was born in Tokyo in 1879, who composed a few songs the most popular ones such as "Hakone-Hachiri" (箱根八里). "Hana" (花, "Flower"), but died unfortuanetly early at the age of 23 from tubercolosis in 1903, "Urami" (憾, "Regret") being the last piece he composed before he passed on.
